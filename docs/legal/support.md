# Expexp — Support

**Expexp** makes film-style double and multiple exposures on your iPhone: stack a few shots into
one luminous image, freeze the scene (or your face) while everything else ghosts by.

## Quick start
1. Open Expexp and allow **Camera** and **Photos** access.
2. Tap the shutter to take your first exposure. Tap it again (2–4 times) for more — a translucent
   ghost of your previous shots shows through so you can line up the next one.
3. Tap the green **✓** to save. Your composite lands in your Photo Library.
4. Tap **✕** to discard a stack and start over.

## Tips
- **Raw vs. Lock:** the bottom-left toggle. **Raw** stacks exactly what you shoot. **Lock** aligns
  your exposures — **Edge Lock** (rear camera) keeps a static scene (like buildings) crisp while
  motion ghosts by; **Face Lock** (front camera) keeps your face steady while the world swirls.
- **The level ring** around the shutter (in Lock mode) helps you re-steady onto your first frame —
  it glows green when you're matched.
- **Looks:** tap **⋯** to switch between Neutral, Filmic, and Moody.
- Works in **portrait and landscape** — just start a stack in the orientation you want.

## Frequently asked
- **Where do my photos go?** Straight to your iPhone's Photo Library when you tap Save. Nothing is
  uploaded anywhere.
- **Do I need an account?** No. There's no sign-up and no data collection.
- **The ghosts get fainter with more shots — is that a bug?** No — that's how film stacking works:
  more exposures means each one is a lighter layer, like a longer shutter.
